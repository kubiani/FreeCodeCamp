Albert Einstein

the most influential physicist of the 20th century

Anyone who has never made a mistake has never tried anything new.

Fast Facts

lived: 14 March 1879 - 17 April 1955

birthplace: Ulm, Württemberg, Germany

Major Discoveries

  • General and special relativity
  • Gravitational waves
  • Photoelectric effect
  • Einstein field equations
  • Unified field theory

Life

  • 1905 - Miracle Year in which he produced 4 groundbreaking articles while working at the patent office.
    1. applied the quantum theory to light to explain the photoelectric effect, in which a material will emit electrically charged particles when hit by light
    2. experimental proof of the existence of atoms, which he got by analyzing the phenomenon of Brownian motion, in which tiny particles were suspended in water
    3. introduced his special theory of relativity, which held that the laws of physics are the same even for objects moving in different inertial frames
    4. concerned the fundamental relationship between mass and energy, concepts viewed previously as completely separate. Einstein’s famous equation E = mc2
  • 1921 - he won the Nobel Prize for physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect
  • 1933 - moved to the US when he was targeted during the rise of Nazi Germany
  • 1939 - Einstein and fellow physicist Leo Szilard wrote to President Franklin D. Roosevelt regarding atomic threat; led to the creation of the Manhattan Project
  • 1945 - bombing of Hiroshima, Japan. Einstein became a major player in efforts to curtail usage of the atomic bomb. Einstein also became a member of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People, seeing the parallels between the treatment of Jews in Germany and African Americans in the United States
